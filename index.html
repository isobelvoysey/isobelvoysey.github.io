About Me

Hi, I'm Isobel and I'm interested in using child-robot interaction for social good. I'm currently a 4th year PhD student at The University of Edinburgh and Heriot-Watt University working on developing a zoomorphic robot for animal welfare education. My supervisors are Dr Michael Herrmann, Prof. Lynne Baillie, and Prof. Joanne Williams.

Before starting my PhD, I completed my BA and MEng in Engineering at Trinity College, Cambridge, with an exchange year spent studying at the National University of Singapore. My master’s project was at the Bio-Inspired Robotics Laboratory under the supervision of Dr Fumiya Iida on autonomous dishwasher loading from cluttered canteen trays.

Alongside my PhD, I enjoy teaching undergrads about robotics and tech ethics, as well as participating in outreach activities to bring robotics and university-style learning to children and teenagers in and around Edinburgh. Outside of my PhD, I like swimming, cooking, and solving cryptic crosswords.
